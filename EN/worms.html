nutrition value

After a long time of searching for the ideal base ingredient for our recipes, we have come up with the mealworm, which contains high amounts of highly digestible protein and other important substances for the body, such as: copper, sodium, potassium, iron, zinc selenium and much more! The protein content is comparable to beef, and the larvae contain high amounts of healthy polyunsaturated fats. It boasts all 10 essential amino acids and omega-3 and omega-6 fats. The special diet of our worms affects their taste and the substances they contain, so they are rich in vitamins and fibre and can taste like chilli.

ecology

Humanity faces many problems, from alarming measurements of CO2 in the atmosphere and dwindling forests that must be cleared to create fertile fields to feed a growing human population. Yet fertile land is dwindling and we are still not able to provide enough food for every human being or child to live a fulfiled life, and even in developed countries, there is already a problem with the lack of good quality clean drinking water.

When in 2050, the global population rises to 9 billion people, there will not be enough water to produce enough food for everyone - if the current Western diet prevails (in the US is consumed about 122 kg of meat/person/year, in the EU 90 kg). It takes 15 500 litres of water to produce one kg of beef, 6.5 kg of grain and 36 kg of roughage. In terms of how much cow drinks, one cow drinks 155 litres per day, a pig 25 litres and a sheep about 20 litres per day. In the last 50 years, world meat consumption has increased 5 times and thus the amount of CO2 has increased accordingly - livestock farming has become one of the biggest emitters of global warming gases, even bigger than the transport or automotive industry.

There are already about 1 billion people starving on our planet - eating a monotonous diet. Today, fields occupied by feeding livestock are third of all the world's cultivated land. This means animal feed and not food for human consumption.

Producing 1kg of mealworm takes 2000 times less water, produces 90 times less CO2, takes up 17 times less space and requires 20 times less feed than 1kg of meat.

processing

The worms are sorted so that we only process the healthy and live ones. The cleaned worms are cooked and thus sterilized. Next, they are added to different vegetable ingredients and adjusted accordingly, flavoured according to the type of final spread. The finished mixture is filled into jars, which are then jarred and prepared for dispatch.
